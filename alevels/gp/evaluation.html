Language--hedges & boosters

If you recall the discussion on balance, hedges such as “may”, “should” and other modal verbs help to convey humility and nuance in your argument. You are conceding to the fact that what you are arguing may not be universally true like the laws of physics, and this lets the markers know that you understand the subtleties of the topic. On the flip side, boosters are words that augment the intensity of your claims and convey conviction in your writing. Examples include “extremely” and “certainly”--appropriate variation of both hedges and boosters in your writing not only adds variety to your language, but also displays your understanding of the realities in the world.

Comparing different contexts & dispositions

If you have read my sample essay in detail, one technique I often used is comparing between different countries, such as developing and developed countries, as this helps you develop your argument based on different contexts. Ultimately, GP is an argumentative essay based on real-life scenarios. As such, it may be wise that one expands on these dispositions to justify their ideas. In fact, many teachers may advise you to write minimally two examples in each paragraph and you can either draw parallels or contrasts between the two examples to extract further insights.

Understanding the tensions of each topic

This segment is arguably the most important aspect of writing a convincing evaluation. Right from the start of my “Introduction to General Paper”, I have been deliberate in telling you to ask yourself this question, “why is this question being asked?” When you are able to fully appreciate this question, you will have mastery over the key tensions of the topic--this will be in the form of the stakeholders involved, the compromises in reality, and the conceptual understandings of the topic. If all these sounds abstract right now, don’t worry! They will begin to concretise as you study the various topics in depth. The purpose of introducing you to these right now is so that you can analyse evidence critically in these angles and extract meaningful insights for your future essays.

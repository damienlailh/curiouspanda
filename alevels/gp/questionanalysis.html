Analysing a past A-level GP question

Before I give you a structured lesson on how to actually write out the essay, such as how many counterarguments or supporting arguments, I just wish to analyse a past A-level question first. I hope to show you that before anyone tells you what is a “right” or “wrong” way of writing an essay, the most appropriate way must make sense to you. As such, I will first walk you through a question so that you can understand why I believe the essay should be structured in a particular way.

2025 A-level Question 5--‘Democracy is the perfect form of government.’ Discuss.

Right off the bat, we can spot a few keywords--democracy, perfect, government and discuss. For some that may be familiar with the idea of balance, the word “discuss” justifies the need for a differing viewpoint from your own. This is why a common way of fulfilling the need for a discussion is to write a counterargument against your stance. The next term we can tackle is “perfect”--this is an absolute statement that should warrant some caution. Do you think it is easier to prove that there are gaps in a democratic system or that it is flawless? As with many things in life, compromises and tradeoffs are ever present and it is inevitable that there will be gaps in any system. In this regard, I would strongly advise, unless other absolute terms are also present, that students refrain from agreeing with absolute statements. Another point I wish to raise is that similar to what I have said in the “Introduction to General Paper”, I like to ask myself, “Why is this question being asked in the first place?” Is it possible that there has been a growing body of arguments that there are cracks in democratic systems?

We now move on to the crux of the question--the link between “democracy” and “government”. If I were to draw an analogy, this is what the question is saying: SARASA pens are the best brand of blue pens. The first thing that may pop up is “what criteria are we judging by?” Similarly, amongst the many forms of government, what metric do we evaluate them? To do so, a natural reaction is to ideate the various objectives that any government has to achieve, and how effective a democracy is in achieving these goals. This requires content knowledge so I will leave it hanging for now, but I hope you will give it some thought and research independently.

Conclusion

If the above paragraphs flew right over your head, the most important takeaway I want to leave you with is simple--answer the question logically! A GP essay is essentially a written debate--you are assessed based on the quality of your arguments so the baseline is always answering the question. This may seem simple, but I need to stress this first before introducing any framework or structure as I have seen far too many people forget their fundamentals.
